What’s the Issue? Deforestation

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of trees which leads to the destruction of forests. This issue affects ecosystems and humans by contributing to climate change, biodiversity loss, and soil degradation.

Causes of Deforestation

  • Urban Construction: Forests are cleared for the expansion of urban areas, including roads, bridges, and power plants, resulting in significant loss of forest life. (PhilCHM)
  • Agriculture: Conversion of forest into agricultural land, including slash-and-burn farming (kaingin) commonly practiced in the Philippines. (PhilCHM)
  • Use for Fuel: Trees are cut down to be used as firewood or turned into charcoal for cooking and heating, especially in rural areas. (PhilCHM)
  • Commercial Purposes: Forests are cleared for oil and mining exploitation, often exacerbated by human-induced fires. (PhilCHM)
  • Illegal Logging: Both legal and illegal logging activities contribute to deforestation, depriving wildlife of their habitat. (PhilCHM)

Effects of Deforestation

  • Erosion: Loss of nutrients from soil leads to dry, infertile land and further environmental degradation. (PhilCHM)
  • Disruption of Water Cycle: Trees help regulate water absorption and release. Deforestation leads to imbalances in the ecosystem’s water cycle. (PhilCHM)
  • Flooding and Drought: Without trees to absorb excess water, periods of flood and drought become more frequent, increasing risks for nearby communities. (PhilCHM)
  • Climate Change: Trees store carbon dioxide. When forests are destroyed, the stored carbon is released back into the atmosphere, exacerbating climate change. (PhilCHM)

Definition of Deforestation

  • The clearing or thinning of forest by humans. (Britannica)
  • The removal and destruction of forests or stands of trees from land, later converted to non-forest use. (Wikipedia)

Current Status of Deforestation in the Philippines

In the Philippines, from 2001 to 2022, a total of 1.42 million hectares of tree cover was lost, representing a 7.6% decrease of the country's total tree cover of approximately 18.684 million hectares. (Climate Change Commission)

From 2002 to 2023, the Philippines lost 190,000 hectares (190 kha) of humid primary forest, which accounts for 13% of its total tree cover loss. During the same period, the total area of humid primary forest decreased by 4.1%. (Global Forest Watch)

What You Can Do

Everyone can play a role in stopping deforestation. Here are some actions you can take:

  • Support reforestation projects
  • Reduce paper and wood consumption
  • Support policies that protect forests
  • Donate to or volunteer with organizations fighting deforestation
